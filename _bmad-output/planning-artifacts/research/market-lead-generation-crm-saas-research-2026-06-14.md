### 1. Problem Validation — Proposal vs. Market

**Proposal claim:** Lead capture happens through separate Google Forms; follow-ups are manual; reports require consolidation; duplicates are hard to detect.

**Market evidence:** Strongly validated.

- Cvent notes Google Forms lacks badge scanning, automatic CRM syncing, and built-in event attribution — teams must export and manually upload to CRM afterward ([Cvent Lead Capture Tools](https://www.cvent.com/en/blog/events/top-lead-capture-tools)).
- Bizzabo identifies the same failure mode: registrations, check-in, and lead data living on disconnected platforms creates attribution gaps and delayed follow-up ([Bizzabo Event Tech Stack Guide](https://www.bizzabo.com/blog/event-tech-stack-crm-integration-guide)).
- Industry commentary on Google Forms → CRM automation cites wasted admin time, data entry errors, delayed follow-ups, and inability to scale as core SMB pain points ([Exponential Scale](https://exponential-scale.com/p/from-your-forms-into-your-crm), [n8n Expert Guide](https://n8n.expert/workflows/automate-data-collection-google-forms-crm/)).
- B2Brain cites that ~70% of booth/event conversations never reach CRM without proper workflow tooling — the proposal's "lost context after registration" narrative aligns with broader lead-capture leakage patterns ([B2Brain Event Lead Capture](https://b2brain.com/event-exhibitors)).

**Implication for client presentation:** The before/after story on pages 2–3 is credible and market-aligned. Consider adding one sentence citing that disconnected form stacks are a known industry failure mode, not just an internal inconvenience.

### 2. Solution Architecture — Activity Engine & QR Flow

**Proposal claim:** Each activity gets a branded registration page + QR code; submissions flow to a master client database linked to activity, community, source, and consent.

**Market evidence:** Core workflow is aligned with 2026 event/lead-capture best practices.

- QR-triggered custom lead forms that route directly to CRM are a standard high-ROI pattern for events and community activations ([Cvent QR Lead Capture](https://www.cvent.com/en/blog/events/increase-your-trade-show-results-lead-capture-qr-code)).
- Unified event tech stacks are moving toward: registration → CRM sync → engagement tracking → reporting in one loop ([Bizzabo](https://www.bizzabo.com/blog/event-tech-stack-crm-integration-guide)).
- QR adoption for events is accelerating; best practice includes dynamic QR codes (repoint URLs without reprinting) and mobile-optimized landing pages ([QR Forever Event Guide 2026](https://qrforever.com/blog/qr-codes-for-events-conferences-trade-shows)).

**Gap vs. market leaders:** The proposal does not mention:
- **Dynamic QR codes** (recommended for recurring activities)
- **Mobile-first form UX** (2026 trend toward conversational/one-question-at-a-time flows showing 30–60% conversion lifts — [Perspective AI](https://getperspective.ai/blog/event-registration-technology-in-2026-6-trends-reshaping-how-attendees-sign-up))
- **Real-time enrichment or scoring** (common in enterprise event tools; appropriately deferred to Phase 2)

**Recommendation:** Keep MVP as-is, but add a discovery-week note: "Registration pages will be mobile-optimized; QR codes will support reuse across recurring activities."

### 3. Master Client Profile & Duplicate Detection

**Proposal claim:** Single profile with contact details, activity-specific answers, relationship history, duplicate checking by contact details.

**Market evidence:** This is the primary value differentiator vs. staying on Google Forms + Sheets.

- CRM market growth is driven by SMEs seeking a **360-degree customer view** and centralized data for personalized follow-up ([Fortune Business Insights CRM Market](https://www.fortunebusinessinsights.com/customer-relationship-management-crm-market-103418)).
- Membership/community tools distinguish themselves from generic CRM by tracking engagement history, event participation, and member lifecycle — not just sales pipeline ([Circle.so Membership vs CRM](https://circle.so/blog/membership-management-software)).

**Risk:** Proposal mentions duplicate checking but not dedup rules (phone normalization, email vs. phone priority, merge workflow). Market implementations typically use email/phone as unique identifiers with explicit merge logic ([Zapier/CRM sync guides](https://deploi.ca/blog/zapier-google-forms-to-crm-sync-integration)).

**Recommendation for Week 1 discovery:** Document dedup policy explicitly in the proposal appendix or SOW.

**Strength:** Page 10 is one of the proposal's strongest market-aligned sections for APAC/community businesses where WhatsApp is primary.

### 7. Competitive Landscape — What the Client Could Buy Instead

The proposal implies a **custom platform** without explicitly comparing alternatives. Market research surfaces four realistic options:

#### Option A: Custom build (Creativorare proposal)
- **Best when:** Activity-specific fields, branded QR per engine, duplicate logic, and community-specific reporting are core to the business model.
- **Market support:** High — generic tools rarely model "Tennis level + clinic interest + referral source" per activity without heavy configuration.

#### Option B: GoHighLevel / agency all-in-one (~$97–297/mo)
- **Strengths:** CRM, funnels, email, SMS, memberships, workflows in one platform ([Nebtrix GHL comparison](https://nebtrix.io/blog/gohighlevel-vs-hubspot-clickfunnels-kajabi-2026)).
- **Weaknesses for this client:** Not activity-engine-native; community features are generic; requires significant setup to replicate three distinct form engines; may be overkill if client doesn't need funnels/SMS at scale.
- **Verdict:** Viable if client accepts configuration over custom UX; less aligned with proposal's "your exact engines" story.

#### Option C: Membership software (Wild Apricot, Join It, Glue Up — ~$29–100+/mo)
- **Strengths:** Event registration, member database, email, reports ([Outseta Membership Software Guide](https://www.outseta.com/posts/best-membership-management-software)).
- **Weaknesses:** Optimized for dues/renewals, not multi-activity lead-engine growth; limited WhatsApp; custom per-activity form logic may still require workarounds.
- **Verdict:** Partial fit for clubs, but weaker on "lead engine" and WhatsApp-first follow-up narrative.

#### Option D: Google Forms + Zapier + lightweight CRM (HubSpot free / Zoho)
- **Strengths:** Lower upfront cost; faster initial setup ([Agent CRM Google Forms integration trend](https://blog.agent-crm.com/google-forms-agent-crm-workflows-native-integration-now-live/)).
- **Weaknesses:** Does not deliver branded activity engine, QR-per-activity management, unified relationship view, or duplicate intelligence — reproduces the fragmentation the proposal solves.
- **Verdict:** Cheaper short-term; fails the stated business outcome.

**Positioning recommendation for client deck:** Add a 1-page "Why a unified platform vs. forms + integrations" comparison. The custom build is justified when **activity-specific capture + WhatsApp-led follow-up + cross-activity client history** are strategic, not administrative.

### Customer Behavior Patterns

Activity-led community operators behave less like traditional B2B sales teams and more like **relationship managers running recurring experiences**. Their "leads" are participants, members, and repeat attendees — not enterprise pipeline deals.

**Behavior drivers:**
- **Capture at peak intent:** Registration happens when someone is already excited enough to sign up; the quality of that moment determines follow-up success ([Perspective AI Event Registration 2026](https://getperspective.ai/blog/online-event-registration-in-2026-a-modern-playbook-for-higher-conversion)).
- **Mobile-first completion:** Form-based registration drops users at field walls; mobile-optimized, low-friction flows materially improve completion ([Perspective AI Registration Apps 2026](https://getperspective.ai/blog/event-registration-apps-in-2026-8-mobile-first-options-compared)).
- **Follow-up speed matters:** Gym/fitness operators report inquiry-to-membership conversion improving dramatically when WhatsApp follow-up happens within minutes, not days ([BetaXLab WhatsApp CRM for Gym](https://www.betaxlab.com/whatsapp-crm-gym)).
- **Referral-driven growth:** Community clubs grow through invitation loops ("invited by," referral source) — behavior segmentation by referral and attendance frequency outperforms age/geo alone ([Club Membership B2B2C Segmentation](https://cricbuzz.news/segment-like-a-pro-bapplying-b2b2c-messaging-tactics-to-grow-)).

**Interaction preferences:**
- **WhatsApp for 1:1 follow-up:** High open/delivery rates vs email for reminders and personal outreach; sport-club CRM vendors cite 94% delivery and ~5× email engagement for WhatsApp ([Rally AI Sport Clubs CRM](https://www.acerally.co.uk/rally-ai)).
- **Email for broadcasts:** Activity announcements, monthly summaries, and promotional campaigns remain email-first in the proposal — aligned with club CRM patterns.
- **QR at point of activity:** Scan-to-register at events/clinics matches 2026 event QR best practice ([Cvent QR Lead Capture](https://www.cvent.com/en/blog/events/increase-your-trade-show-results-lead-capture-qr-code), [QR Forever Events Guide 2026](https://qrforever.com/blog/qr-codes-for-events-conferences-trade-shows)).

**Decision habits:**
- Operators approve tools that mirror **existing workflows** (forms, WhatsApp, weekly activity rhythm) rather than abstract CRM concepts.
- Success is judged by **registrations, repeat participation, follow-up coverage, and activity ranking** — matching the proposal dashboard (pages 8–9).
- Attendance gap is structural: cross-industry ~49–56% of registrants may not attend live events; platforms that capture intent and enable follow-up outperform raw signup counts ([Perspective AI Registration Platforms 2026](https://getperspective.ai/blog/online-event-registration-platforms-10-picks-for-2026-by-event-type)).

### Customer Interaction Patterns

**Research and discovery:**
- Participants discover activities via Instagram, friend invite, or QR at venue — not CRM comparison shopping
- Operators discover solutions via agency referral (Creativorare), peer recommendation, or pain-triggered search ("Google Forms alternative for events")

**Purchase decision process (operator/buyer):**
- SMB CRM buyers prioritize **ease of use (25%), TCO (25%), workflow fit (20%)** over feature breadth ([Authencio CRM Requirements](https://www.authencio.com/blog/crm-requirements-checklist-features-scoring-templates))
- Typical evaluation: 2-week trial with real data, 3 candidates max, team reaction weighs heavily ([Easyly CRM Checklist](https://www.easyly.com/blog/how-to-pick-a-crm))
- For custom-build proposals: decision hinges on **vendor understanding of existing forms + clear MVP scope + timeline** — not G2 ratings

**Post-"purchase" (launch) behavior:**
- Phased rollout beats big-bang launch; incremental adoption improves retention ([FindMyCRM Adoption Blueprint](https://www.findmycrm.com/blog/crm-adoption-blueprint-for-small-teams))
- Operators need quick time-to-value: dashboard live, first QR engine running, first weekly report within weeks 6–8

**Loyalty and retention:**
- Loyalty in community businesses = repeat attendance + referral + consent to messaging ([Axrail WhatsApp Loyalty](https://www.axrail.ai/post/whatsapp-loyalty-customer-retention))
- Inactive-client detection and win-back campaigns are Phase 2 high-ROI features
